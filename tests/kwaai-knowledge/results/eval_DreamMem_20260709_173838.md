## Answers

### q01 — What is the Overfitted Brain Hypothesis, and who proposed it?

The Overfitted Brain Hypothesis (OBH) posits that dreams help mitigate the problem of overfitting in the brain. According to this hypothesis, the brain is constantly at risk of overfitting when its learning is based too much on a particular data set, and dreams help improve generalizability and robustness by generating data far outside the organism's daily "training set" in a warped or corrupted way [16]. 

The OBH was not attributed to a specific person who proposed it. However, it was formally outlined and developed in the paper referenced as [17], which surveyed existing evidence within both neuroscience and deep learning and put forward testable predictions that can be pursued both in vivo and in silico.

### q02 — According to Hoel's paper, what evolutionary function do dreams serve in relation to machine-learning-style overfitting?
