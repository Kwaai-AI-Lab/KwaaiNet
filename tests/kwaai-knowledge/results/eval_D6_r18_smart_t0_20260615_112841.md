Many families were torn apart, and people were relocated to other areas, often in poverty-stricken conditions. The community was destroyed, and many of its residents were left without access to basic amenities like housing, healthcare, and education.

The Group Areas Act also had a profound impact on the economy and social fabric of District Six. The act was a key component of the apartheid regime's policies of racial segregation and oppression, which aimed to maintain white dominance over South Africa.
