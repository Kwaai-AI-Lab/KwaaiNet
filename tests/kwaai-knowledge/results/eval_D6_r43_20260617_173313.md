### q19 — What was the Non-European Unity Movement?

The Non-European Unity Movement (NEUM) was a South African political movement founded in 1943 that united non-European (African, Coloured, and Indian) political organizations opposed to apartheid and racial discrimination [1]. Its program was based on Ten Point demands for full democratic rights. The NEUM adopted a boycott policy – non-collaboration with apartheid government institutions such as the Coloured Advisory Council, apartheid school boards, and separate development bodies. 

The Unity Movement was closely affiliated with other organizations including the All African Convention (AAC), the Teachers League of South Africa (TLSA), and the New Era Fellowship (NEF) [1].

The movement's Ten Point Programme included demands for:

* The full unfettered franchise – the right to elect and be elected to all councils of the state
* Free and equal education to the age of sixteen
* Penal reforms
* Workers' rights
* The right to buy and sell land, with a new division of the land as the first act of a democratic parliament [4].

The Unity Movement aimed to politicize existing non-political organizations such as sports bodies, churches, charities, mothers' unions, rate-payers and civics, and bring them into the movement [7].
