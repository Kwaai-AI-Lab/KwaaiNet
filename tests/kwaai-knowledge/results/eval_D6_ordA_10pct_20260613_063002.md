### q34 — What was the Group Areas Act and how did it affect District Six?

The Group Areas Act was a law passed in 1950 by the Nationalist government in South Africa that aimed to segregate people according to their race. The act was used to forcibly remove non-White communities from areas designated as White, and to reclassify existing neighborhoods into separate racial groupings.

According to sources like [14], the Group Areas Act was a "crowning piece of the Government legislation" and a "Big Bertha" in the Apartheid arsenal. The act's main goal was to classify people according to their race, or to put it in today's terminology, "colour code" them.

The law had significant consequences for District Six, which was a thriving multi-racial community in Cape Town. One of the notable effects was that the community's complex family structures were not taken into account by the authorities ([8]).
