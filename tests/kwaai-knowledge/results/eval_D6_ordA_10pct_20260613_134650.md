### q10 — Tell me about Kloof Nek.

According to the provided sources, Kloof Nek was a connecting saddle of land that joined Table Mountain to Lion's Head, located in Cape Town. It sprawled protectively on the eastern flank of the city with its head gazing inscrutably down on Table Bay (Source [1]). The area was known for its natural beauty and was frequented by lovers, mountaineers, nature lovers, ramblers, and others who appreciated the scenic views (Source [3]).

The author recalls visiting Kloof Nek as a child with a friend, who showed him the area as an ideal place to study poetry before their literature exams (Source [1]). The two friends spent time in the gardens of Kloof Nek, reading Gray's Elegy and Milton's Lycidas, which seemed fitting for the area's beauty (Sources [3] and [19]).

Kloof Nek was also notable for its cavelets, culverts, and bushes that provided refuge for people who struggled financially or had no place to live. These individuals were often referred to as "Bergies" (Source [3]).
