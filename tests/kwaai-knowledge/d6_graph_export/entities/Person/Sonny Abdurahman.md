Sonny Abdurahman was a prominent figure in the D6 community during the apartheid era. He played a key role in organizing protests against train apartheid, including a large gathering on the Grand Parade where he called for a volunteer force to court arrest by sitting in coaches restricted to Europeans Only. His call to action was met with loud applause from the crowd, who then proceeded to use the whole train quietly as instructed. Despite his involvement in these efforts, Abdurahman was found guilty of inciting violence and fined £5.
