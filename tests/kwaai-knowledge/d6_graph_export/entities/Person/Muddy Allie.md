Muddy Allie was a South African cricket player from the mid-20th century who played for the Kismets C.C. and was part of the Western Province side that competed against Eastern Province. He was known to be a skilled table tennis player, particularly with his windmilling forehand. Muddy's contributions to the D6 community were significant, as he was one of the few players from the Indian Union who broke down sectarian barriers in cricket. His friendship with Mike Allie and involvement in various matches, including against the Yorkshire Cricket Club, demonstrate his dedication to the sport.
