General Nguyen Giap was a Vietnamese military leader and key figure in the Vietnam People's Army during the First Indochina War. He is most notable for his strategic victory at Dien Bien Phu, where he led the army to defeat the French stronghold after a prolonged battle lasting several months. This significant win marked a turning point in the war, with over 10,000 prisoners taken and a humiliating defeat for the French. General Giap's military prowess and leadership played a crucial role in shaping the course of the conflict.
