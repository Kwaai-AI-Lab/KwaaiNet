Fazil was a member of a family living in District Six during the apartheid era. He played an important role in his family's life, particularly as a younger sibling who looked up to his older brother for stories and guidance. Fazil was also involved in significant events such as the move to a new property at 24 Queens Road, Woodstock, which marked the first time since their grandfather's era that the family would become property owners. This event had a profound impact on Fazil's understanding of property ownership and its limitations.
