Jan Christiaan Smuts was a prominent South African politician and statesman of the early 20th century, serving as the Prime Minister of South Africa during World War II. He played a significant role in shaping the country's foreign policy and was a key figure in the formation of the United Nations. Smuts was also known for his views on segregation and apartheid, which he expressed in his Christmas message to the nation in 1941, where he stated that 'Segregation has fallen on evil days.' He was a member of the Imperial War Cabinet and contemplated the possibility of a Japanese invasion with utmost gravity.
