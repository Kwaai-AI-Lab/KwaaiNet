Wilson was a key figure in late Victorian Cape Town, serving as a sports official and politician. He was involved in various events and meetings, including chairing the first Progressive Party meeting in August 1898. Wilson also spoke out against 'class' legislation and predicted that if South Africa were united, coloured people would have to be sacrificed.
