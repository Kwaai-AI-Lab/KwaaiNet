The South African Indians were a group of people who made up about half a million of the population in Natal, with a significant portion being working class or poor peasants. Despite facing numerous challenges and odds against them, they had also achieved considerable wealth through their ability to succeed and prosper as a merchant class, which was a source of bitter envy among the Natal Whites.
