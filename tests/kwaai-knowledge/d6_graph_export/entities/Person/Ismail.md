Ismail was a tailor from District Six who continued to charge prewar prices for his suits despite the economic challenges faced by the community. He was known for his strong personality and his ability to mimic the Piccadilly accent superbly, which he used to pillory his opponents in public debates. Ismail's battles with Cissie Gool were legendary, and he was a key figure in the Coloured Affairs Department controversy. His legacy as a fierce advocate for the rights of the community continues to be celebrated.
