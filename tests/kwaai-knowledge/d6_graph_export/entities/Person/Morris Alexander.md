Morris Alexander was a resident of District Six during the apartheid era. He played a significant role in the community as a neighbor and acquaintance to various individuals mentioned in the text, including Gandhi and Hanief. Morris's home was visited by Gandhi on one occasion, where he stayed overnight and received gifts and addresses from the community. Morris also interacted with the narrator, who considered him a friend and playmate.
