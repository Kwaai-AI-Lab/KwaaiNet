Kies was a key figure in the D6 community, playing a significant role in the Anti-CAD Movement and the Unity Movement. He was known for his strong convictions and unwavering commitment to social justice. Kies was also involved in various events and meetings, including the Land Tenure Advisory Board meeting in Cape Town, where he presented a plea to put aside personal differences for the sake of the Movement. His characterisation of the conflict between the Capitalist West and the Soviet Bloc as a conflict between two camps was met with criticism from Tabata.
