Zobeida was a Cape Malay/Indian woman who lived in South Africa during the early 20th century and made significant contributions to education by becoming one of the first girls from her community to qualify as a Bachelor of Arts. She achieved this partly at Fort Hare, a university that catered largely for Xhosa speaking Africans.
