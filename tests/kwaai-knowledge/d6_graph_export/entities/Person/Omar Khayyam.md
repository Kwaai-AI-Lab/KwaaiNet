Omar Khayyam was a renowned Persian poet from the 11th century, best known for his Rubaiyat poems that have been widely translated and admired for their philosophical and mystical themes. His works often explored the human condition, mortality, and the fleeting nature of life. The Rubaiyat's verses were frequently referenced in the memoirs of Yousuf (Joe) Rassool, reflecting its significant cultural impact. Khayyam's poetry has been a source of inspiration for many, including those who lived through the tumultuous times of apartheid South Africa.
