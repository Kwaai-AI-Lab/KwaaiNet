Dominion Status refers to a political status granted by the British government to certain countries in Africa and Asia after World War II, allowing them to maintain their independence while still being under British dominion. This concept was significant in the context of the source text as it relates to the lives and works of notable individuals listed, such as Dr. Hendrik Frensch Verwoerd, who served as Prime Minister of South Africa during the country's transition from Dominion Status to Republic status.
