The Socialist Camp refers to a geopolitical concept that emerged in the Cold War era, describing the socialist states aligned with the Soviet Union as a unified front against the Western Bloc and imperialism. This concept was used by various organizations and individuals to describe the ideological and political opposition to capitalism and the Western powers.
