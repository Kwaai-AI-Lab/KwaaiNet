Cape to Cairo refers to a historical concept that represents the idea of extending British colonial control from Cape Town in South Africa to Cairo in Egypt. In the context of the source text, it is used to describe Cecil John Rhodes' imperialistic ambitions and is also referenced as the brand name of cigarettes smoked by the narrator's grandfather.
